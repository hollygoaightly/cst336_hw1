Augusta Ada King, Countess of Lovelace was an English mathemetician and writer from the 19th century.

Tutored in math from an early age, her academic career lead to her aquaintance with Charles Babbage.

This lead to her involvement in the design of the first general-purpose computer that could be described in modern terms as Turing-complete.

In her work, Lovelace believed that the machine had further applications beyond arithmetic and wrote the first algorithm intended for a computer.
