Grace Brewster Murray Hopper was born in 1906 and grew up to become an American computer scientist and United States Navy rear admiral.

She was one of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I computer, The IBM Automatic Sequence Controlled Calculator (ASCC).

Hooper also invented one of the first linkers, or program compiler that produces a single executable.

She also put forth the first ever theory of machine-independent programming languages, and then went on to create the FLOW-MATIC programming language, which would be further extended into the high level programming language COBOL.
